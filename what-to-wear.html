The whole wedding has an informal vibe, but hey, it’s a wedding, so even hyper-casual Amy is making an effort. Unlike the bride, you don’t have to have three hair trials before the day, but you too might want to eschew your jeans and trainers for something more smart-casual (or casual-smart).

Gents, kilts and collars are encouraged; ties and jackets optional. Ladies, dress up to let your hair down. If you’re looking for any sort of pointers on colour scheme, Raymond’s tartan is Duncan Ancient, so you could pick out colours from that.

Dress for the weather too: we plan to get married outside unless it’s bucketing down, and there will be a short distance to walk on grass. Some (taller, younger) guests will be required to stand during the ceremony.
